# Trust Signals: A Principle-Based Design Guide

Strategic trust signal design — not copy-paste patterns.

---

## 1. Trust Psychology

Trust signals reduce perceived risk at the moment of decision. They answer: "Is this safe?"

**When trust matters most**: High stakes, first-time visitors, point of commitment, competitive markets.

**Diminishing returns**: 3-4 strong signals beat 10 weak ones. One verified credential beats five unverifiable badges.

---

## 2. The Trust Hierarchy

### Tier 1: Specific, Verifiable Evidence (Strongest)
Can be fact-checked. "QBCC License #1234567", "4.8 stars (127 Google Reviews)" + link, "Serving Brisbane since 1998."

### Tier 2: Third-Party Validation
Someone else vouched. Real testimonials with full names, client logos, industry awards, press mentions.

### Tier 3: Self-Claimed Credentials
Credible but self-reported. "Fully licensed and insured", "Family owned", "Background checked."

### Tier 4: Generic Assurances (Weakest)
"Quality guaranteed", "Friendly service", "Fast response."

**Rule**: Always prefer one Tier 1 signal over three Tier 4 signals.

---

## 3. Context-Sensitive Trust

| Business Type | What Matters | Placement |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Emergency services | Speed, availability, local | Hero, near phone |
| Professional services | Credentials, experience | Mid-page, team bios |
| E-commerce | Reviews, returns, security | Product pages, checkout |
| Local trades | Licence, insurance, longevity | Footer, near contact |

---

## 4. Lead-Gen vs Client Sites

### Client Sites (Real Business Data)
Use Tier 1-2 aggressively. Specificity builds authority.

### Lead-Gen Sites (No Real Customers)
Use Tier 3-4 only. Focus on process and promises.

**NEVER fabricate on lead-gen**: Star ratings, years in business, licence numbers, named individuals, customer counts.

| Do Say | Don't Say |
|--------|-----------|
| "Experienced Team" | "25 Years Experience" |
| "Highly Rated" | "4.9 Stars (127 Reviews)" |
| "Licensed & Insured" | "QBCC License #1234567" |

---

## 5. Placement Strategy

- **Near CTAs**: Reduces friction at decision point
- **Early on page**: First impression reassurance
- **In context**: Paired with relevant service content
- **Footer**: Expected location for credentials

**Don't**: Sticky header bars, every page same spot, modal popups, above hero content.

---

## 6. Anti-Sameness

Trust doesn't need its own section. Distribute throughout:
- Hero subheadline: "QBCC Licensed Plumbers Serving Gold Coast Since 1998"
- Service descriptions: "Gas Safe certified technicians..."
- Near CTA: "No Call-Out Fee"
- Footer: Licence numbers and memberships

One powerful proof point beats six generic ones.

---

## 7. What to NEVER Do

- Fabricate specifics
- Use stock badge icons (generic shield with checkmark)
- Copy-paste same trust section on every page
- Lead with trust before main message (signals insecurity)
