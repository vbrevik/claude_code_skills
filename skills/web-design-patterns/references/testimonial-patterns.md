# Testimonial Design Principles

Design principles for social proof that persuades without feeling manipulative or templated.

---

## 1. Social Proof Psychology

- **Specificity beats generality**: "Fixed our leak in 2 hours" > "Great service!"
- **Named, identifiable people are more credible**: "Sarah Mitchell, Homeowner in Maitland" > "John D."
- **Relevant context matters**: Match reviewer's situation to visitor's situation
- **Quality over quantity**: One detailed story beats ten "5 stars great service"

---

## 2. Lead-Gen vs Client Sites

### Client Sites (Real Customers)
Pull from actual reviews (Google, Facebook). Use real names and photos. Show verification: platform link, star rating, review date.

### Lead-Gen Sites (No Real Customers)
**DO NOT fabricate testimonials.**

Use instead: Service promise statements, platform badges (even with zero reviews), trust signals, anonymised case studies, founder story.

---

## 3. Design Approach Selection

### Single Featured Quote
When: One powerful, detailed testimonial. Make it big, editorial, impactful.

### Grid of Quotes
When: 3-6 good testimonials. Vary formatting — one featured, rest standard. Mix with/without photos. Different lengths create rhythm.

### Integrated Pull Quotes
When: Long-form content. Position near relevant content. Feels most natural.

### Logo Wall + Quotes
When: B2B, recognisable client brands. Grey out logos for subtlety.

### NO CAROUSELS
Users see 1 of 5 testimonials, <1% click controls. Show all or curate best 3.

---

## 4. Content Principles

- **Keep it brief**: Brief quotes get read; paragraphs get skipped
- **Include the problem, not just praise**: Problem + solution = story
- **Star ratings only if real and verifiable**: Link to source
- **Photos**: Real = huge trust boost. Stock = huge trust loss. None > fake.
- **Attribution**: Full name + location + context minimum. Never "A satisfied customer."

---

## 5. Placement

- **After presenting services**: You've explained, now prove it works
- **Near CTA sections**: Reduce last-minute doubt
- **Within service descriptions**: Pull quote format
- **Not necessarily in their own section**: Context-relevant placement works better

---

## 6. Anti-Patterns

- Auto-rotating carousels
- All testimonials saying the same thing (curate for variety)
- Stock photo headshots
- "John D." level anonymity
- Three identical card-shaped testimonials
- All 5-star ratings (4.7 is more believable than 5.0)

---

## 7. When You Don't Have Testimonials

Don't fake them. Use: credentials, guarantees, process descriptions, review platform links, anonymised case studies, founder story.

| Situation | Recommended Approach |
|-----------|---------------------|
| One powerful testimonial | Single featured quote, make it big |
| 3-6 good testimonials | Grid with variety, one featured |
| Many reviews (20+) | Curate best 5-6 + link to "see all on Google" |
| No real testimonials | Service promises, guarantees, process |
| Lead-gen site | Trust signals, link to empty review profile |
